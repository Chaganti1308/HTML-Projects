Swami Vivekananda


Swami Vivekananda, a key figure in the introduction of indian philosophies of Vedanta and Yoga to the westren world, played a crucial role in the arrival of Hinduism in India.His inspirational speeches and teachings continue to influence and inspire people around the globe.

About the Legend

  • Swami Vivekananda was born as Narendranath Datta on January12, 1863, in Kolkata, India
  • He was a brilliant student and had a keen interest in spirituality from young age.
  • Vivekananda became is a disciple of the saint Ramakrishna, who taught him that all living being were an embodiment of the divine self
  • He is best known for his speech at the parliament of world's religious in Chicago, in 1893, where he began his speech with "Brothers and sisters of America....."
  • Vivekananda founded the Ramakrishna Mission in 1897, which works in the area of education, healthcare and social service.
  • His teachings focused on the potential diversity of every individual and the idea of serving humanity as a way to serve God.
  • Swami Vivekananda books on four Yogas(Raja Yoga, Karma Yoga, Bhakti Yoga, Jnana Yoga) are widely rewarded and read.
  • He passed on July 04, 1902, at the young age of 39, but his legacy continuos to lives on through his teaching and the institutions he established.
